Example Structure: Return and New World

❗ Motivation and Definition

The Return and the New World are the ultimate points of contrast against the Ordinary World. This is where the characters make some kind of physical or symbolic journey back to where they started, create change, and then reflect on the true consequences of that change. You might think of it as the 'happily ever after', the final statement on the idea you are trying to convey to your audience.

❓ Caveats

Not all stories have a 'happily ever after'. To convey their desired ideas, they might end negatively -- the change may not be accepted for instance, or the change may not work in the way that the characters hoped. Some stories even neglect the New World entirely when interpreted with this structure (e.g. the movie The Platform does this for ideological effect).

And as a final statement on this structure, any of these segments can be neglected, skimmed, or told out of order. Because structures like this are so common, a knowing audience will likely fill in the gaps and rearrange the pieces intuitively.
