Example Structure: Trials

❗ Motivation and Definition

You can understand Trials as 'the part in the middle, before the characters have to sacrifice something, where they might talk, fight, and solve problems'. I like to understand each Trial as a little story in and of itself, each with its own Ordinary World, Call, etc., right through to New World, wherein the New World directly moves the characters closer to or further away from the change they want to achieve. This helps each Trial feel meaningful to the overall story.

❓ Caveats

Understanding the struggles that characters face as individual problems to be solved might feel too disjointed for your story. Some might need to weave in and out of each other nebulously, stay unresolved, or produce no change at all for your story to be most effective at conveying its ideas.
