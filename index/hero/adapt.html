Example Structure: Adapt

❗ Motivation and Definition

This structure is all about change, and so, all about contrast: contrasting the Ordinary World with the New World, contrasting the characters' desires in the Ordinary World with the characters' desires in The Call, etc. Adapt, as the section where the characters have peak motivation and trust in their goal, might serve as an interesting contrast in both directions. It might contrast the characters' uncertainty and immaturity in the Trials that came before. And it might contrast the characters' lowest point in The Abyss that is to come. By providing different points of contrast throughout your story, you might make each change feel more meaningful and create a general ebb and flow that guides the reader through your story.

❓ Caveats

Your story might not be about contrast at all. Or maybe, your characters simply get less and less motivated until they hit The Abyss.
